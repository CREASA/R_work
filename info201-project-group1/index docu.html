On August 3rd, 1949, BAA changed its name to NBA after merging with the competing National Basketball League (NBL). Nowadays, the NBA has become the most popular sports league in the United States, sharing the equal popularity with the NFL and the MLB. The NBA regular season runs from October to April, with each team playing 82 games and the top 8 teams of northern and western parts will enroll in a playoff tournament to compete for the final championship. The playoff tournament extends into June. However, the 2019 to 2020 NBA season was the special case. It began on October 22, 2019, and originally was supposed to end on April 15th, 2020. Due to the COVOID-19 pandemic, the season had to be suspended on March 11th.

Dataset

ASA All NBA Raw Data

This data set includes all the NBA games in Regular seasons and Playoff seasons: scores of each 2 teams, detailed data statistics of every player in the game. We download the data from the official NBA websites of Data Analysis, and the link is to the NBA RAW DATA. Data comes from the official staff of NBA union who records the data of every game and stores them into this data set, and the computer also makes the data more logical and reliable with specific analysis. Actually most of the questions we post are likely to be answered by this data set since it includes tons of valuable data for us to analyze.

NBA Players stats since 1950

The data-set contains aggregate individual statistics for 67 NBA seasons, from basic box-score attributes such as points, assists, rebounds etc., to more advanced money-ball like features such as Value Over Replacement. We downloaded from Kaggle, and the url is NBA Players stats since 1950. The data is collected by Omri Goldstein, who is a Data Scientist at Walkme. He scraped the raw data from Basketball-reference and banded them together by himself.The data includes all of the basic information of players, including the physical data and their educational background, such as their graduated university. With these data, we are able to analysis tasks such as the relationships between players quality and their universities and relationships between players physical data and their scoring efficiency.

Does a taller player perform better?

A common thinking towards basketball is that taller people tend to take more advantage on the court,so I used the scatterplot to investigate whether such relationship exists in NBA, and the it turns out that there is no direct relationships between these two factors. Even though there is a small peak when players' height is 210 cm on the trend line, there are still too much outliers exist. The reason for this is that full-court basketball is a team sport, and different players serve different positions and functions depending on their skills and physical quality, so being short does not mean that he cannot take advantage during the game.But this result might change if people are playing one-on-one. The variables I used for this problem is players height, their EFG rate, and the WS rate during the game. The EFG refers to effective field goal percentage,which evaluates the real field goal percentage of players during the game. The WS refers to how much they contributed for winning the game.

Do people who have longer career experiences play better than people with less experience?

The opinion which experienced NBA players perform better on the floor than the rookies seems to convince most NBA fans since rookie players are likely to make more mistakes. Based on our investigate of comparing WS and PER value of rookie and experienced, the average WS and PER value of the experienced players are a little bit higher than the rookies but not with a huge gap, and some rookies have pretty high WS and PER. Based on that, the answer to such a question is experienced NBA players do perform better than the rookies, but talent matters as well.

Do centers have lower free throw percentages than power forwards?

In this graph, centers are shown in green color, and power forwards are shown in orange color. Free throw percentage is determined by dividing the number of free throws by the number of free-throw attempts. Therefore, free throws are on the Y-axis and free throw attempts are on the X-axis. The maximum free throw percentage in the Center position is 1, the minimum free throw percentage is 0, the medium is 0.75, and the mean is 0.6791036. In comparison, the maximum in power forward is 1, the medium is 0.75, the mean is 0.7146871, and the minimum is 0. According to statistics shown in the table, there are centers and forwards with a maximum free-throw percentage of 1, a minimum of 0, and a medium of 0.75. Therefore, peoples assumption in power forwards is always better than centers in free throw is not entirely true because there are exceptions. For some players, there may be confounding variables involved in the assumption, such as the players practice time, mood, or fatigue in the game. People should not just make conclusions for everyone just based on their position. Furthermore, we can conclude that most of the power forwards have a better free throw percentage than centers because the mean is higher, with a 0.7146871 compare to 0.6791036. A 0.0355835 of difference. As ft and fta increase, there are fewer centers (green points) than power forwards (orange points). By looking at the graph, it is hard to tell which position has a better free throw percentage, but we can see that as the x-axis and y-axis increase, more power forwards appear on the graph.
